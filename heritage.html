Preserving Our Heritage

Walking through the corridors of time in a historic city.

UNESCO Recognition

Melaka was inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site on 7 July 2008, recognized for its unique architectural and cultural townscape without parallel anywhere in East and Southeast Asia.

Architectural Landmarks

The Stadthuys

Built in 1650 as the official residence of Dutch Governors, it is believed to be the oldest remaining Dutch historical building in the Orient. Today, it houses the Museum of History and Ethnography.

A'Famosa Fort

The Porta de Santiago is all that remains of the once-mighty Portuguese fortress built in 1511. It stands as a silent witness to the various colonial shifts that shaped the region.

Flor de la Mar

Intangible Heritage

Heritage in Melaka is not just about buildings; it is found in the living traditions that have been passed down through generations. These include:

  • Traditional Craftsmanship: The art of hand-making beaded shoes (Kasut Manek) and traditional Nyonya embroidery.
  • Oral Traditions: Folk tales of Hang Tuah and the Malay Sultanate that continue to inspire local literature.
  • Culinary Heritage: Ancient recipes of the Kristang and Peranakan communities that use ingredients unique to the Straits.
